Md Shamim Hussain

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) in New York, where I also received my Master of Science (MS) degree in Computer Science. My research focuses on attention-based deep neural networks and their application to graphs, and I am passionate about exploring and developing novel techniques in this area.

I am a recipient of the RPI-IBM AI Horizons Scholarship and currently working under the combined supervision of my academic advisor, Dr. Mohammed J Zaki, and my IBM mentor, Dr. Dharmashankar Subramanian. I had the opportunity to participate in two summer residencies at IBM T.J. Watson Research Center, where I worked on improving the efficiency and generalization of transformer neural networks, exploring new graph transformer architectures and their use in accurate molecular property prediction. I have published in prestigious conferences such as KDD'22, KDD'23 and ICML'24, and served as a peer reviewer for notable journals including IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems and IEEE Transactions on Big Data.

I received a B.Sc. and M.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), Dhaka. Prior to starting my Ph.D., I worked as a Research Engineer developing a deep convolutional neural network based breast tumor classification system.

I am always looking for new challenges and opportunities to advance my knowledge and contribute to the field of computer science.
